At the time of writing, I have just got the core of the website back up and running after a good 6 months or so of downtime. This downtime was caused by [hugo](https://github.com/gohugoio/hugo), the static site generator I use, turning to AI slop machines. I dropped it shortly after finding out, and left the content alone.

After procrastinating for a while, I started writing [violet](https://codeberg.org/LunarWatcher/violet), because I wanted to get a website back up. Raw HTML has never been an option; I need my templates and markdown files for larger-scale site building without losing my mind. Tried that before, and I'm not doing it again.

In the last couple months or so before the return of the site, I've been looking at a good few personal sites outside my "standard sphere", largely driven by stumbling into 88x31s and webrings. Having spent a good few more hours than I'd like to admit seeing the everything but standard-looking websites people have made influenced me somewhat. This revision is what came of that. It's not all that different from the previous version of this site for now, but I have a few explicit goals I'd like to do:

1. I'd like some unhinged shit on the front page
2. ... and I'd like to actually write an "about me" that isn't atrocious. I have tried and failed at this for about as long as I've had fields in which I can describe myself
  * This includes adding a section for additional 88x31s with just stuff I stand by or whatever
3. I'd like to join some webrings
4. Long-term: I'd like a section of arbitrary HTML/CSS/JS stuff&trade; - there's so much weird shit that can be made just for the shits and giggles, and I need to contribute to that

## Extended motivation


Two years ago (sheesh, time flies), at the start of the AI hype wave, [Kyle Hill published a video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JrcbH0ge2WE) that still resonates with me the more time goes on, and [the bigger the list of open slopware gets](https://codeberg.org/small-hack/open-slopware). The internet I knew is getting overrun with AI slop, and there doesn't appear to be a way back. At least not for now.

In most previous iterations of this site, I have leaned towards the "standard" website design principles used in corporate web design, in part because that's what all my reference points looked like. I still somewhat prefer this approach, because it lets me deal with certain things that are important to me; accessibility and usability. That's part of why this site actually doesn't set a font; it does what I'd argue most of the web should do, and respects the default fonts set in your browser settings. A goal in itself is trying to combine this approach with actual personalisation, which is a design challenge. Most of this is a design challenge really, and I'm not a designer.

I still want to combine this with something personal that breaks from the more standard web conventions, because it's fun, interesting, and is a category of design I have slightly more of a chance to not make atrocities with.

I suppose my motivation can be summarised as a desire to make something that leans more towards the small web rather than something that's inspired by the commercial web. Leaning that way in previous iterations of this site wasn't intentional, but a consequence of search engines making the tiny personal websites disappear. This is why webrings helped me with my motivation - seeing sites made by real people with real creativity, rather than whatever fucking slop design system Apple, Google, or Microslop has hyped up this design cycle, helps me at least want to be more creative.

I also stumbled into [the 250KB club](https://250kb.club/) at some point, and [neocities](https://neocities.org/), though the context that made the existence of neocities interesting has been lost to my browser history's awful search (pretty sure it was [this YouTube video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tkUgOT22F5s)).

Whether I actually can be creative is another can of worms, but I won't find out if I don't have the motivation to try :)
